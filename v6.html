V6 Hybrid Explained

The V6 Hybrid combines a 6 cylinder internal combustion engine with electric energy recovery systems. These systems collect energy from braking and heat then turn it into extra power stored via the battery.

Hamilton/Mercedes and Verstappen/Red Bull Racing

Lewis Hamilton and Mercedes dominated the early portion of the V6 Hybrid era. Mercedes won 8 constructors championships and along those, Hamilton won 6 driver championships. From 2021 to 2024, Max Verstappen dominated the season, winning 4 consecutive driver championships with Red Bull Racing winning 2 constructor championships along the way.

Memorable Race

The 2021 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix is considered to be one of the most controversial races ever. A late safety car allowed Max Verstappen's Red Bull car to pit and gain a fresh set of new tires then, the race director made a controversial decision to allow the cars between Hamilton and Verstappen to separate, allowing Verstappen to be the car directly behind Hamilton. Verstappen passed him on the final lap and won his first drivers championship.

Fun Fact

The engines are able to convert its fuel energy into power for it's electrical systems. This gives the car extra horse power and makes it go significantly faster.

Signifiance of the V6 Hybrid

The V6 Hybrid engines were significant because they introduced advanced hybrid technology to F1. These engines comvined traditional combustion engines with energy recovery systems that produce electrical power, pushing the sport toward greater efficiency and innovation. This era is also defined by the dominance of Lewis Hamilton and Mercedes as well as Max Verstappen and Red Bull Racing. 2014 - Present.
